# Client Work -- SBDC Marketing Advisory Templates

## About This Work

I am a Marketing Advisor at the NorCal/East Bay Small Business Development
Center (SBDC), where I provide free, confidential one-on-one marketing
advising to small business owners across the East Bay. I hold an MS in
Marketing Analytics from CSU East Bay and a BA in Marketing from San Jose
State University.

The SBDC's mandate is advising, not service delivery: my role is to teach
frameworks and build client capacity, not to produce finished work a client
would otherwise pay an agency for. Every template in this folder reflects
that philosophy -- they are structured for clients to fill in, execute, and
own themselves, not polished deliverables I hand off complete.

Note on confidentiality: SBDC clients sign a confidentiality agreement as
part of intake. Nothing in this repository identifies a specific client,
business, or session. Everything here is a generalized, reusable template
or framework drawn from patterns across many engagements.

## What's in This Folder

Templates and frameworks I use across client engagements, including:

- **Website and SEO audit templates** -- plain-language frameworks for
  reviewing a small business website (title tags, meta descriptions, H1
  structure, alt text, structured data) and translating technical findings
  into prioritized, non-technical action items.

- **Marketing strategy one-pagers** -- a consistent format for presenting
  target audience, unique value proposition, channel priorities, and a
  30-day action plan in a single reference document.

- **Social media content calendar templates** -- multi-week post planning
  frameworks with caption prompts and hashtag guidance the client fills in,
  built around platform-specific limits (e.g. Instagram's 5-hashtag cap).

- **CRM and tool adoption walkthroughs** -- step-by-step setup guides for
  free-tier tools like Zoho CRM and Buffer, written for non-technical small
  business owners with no prior CRM or scheduling tool experience.

- **Marketing workbook templates** -- intake-style worksheets that help a
  client articulate their UVP, target audience, competitive positioning,
  and channel priorities in their own words.

- **Government procurement guides** -- frameworks for small contractors and
  service businesses navigating local/county vendor registration and
  certification processes.

## Methodology

Every deliverable is built to be executed by the client, not for the
client. Frameworks include explicit instructions, prompts, and placeholder
fields rather than finished copy. The goal is durable capacity, not a
one-time output.
